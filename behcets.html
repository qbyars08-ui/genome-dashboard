Section 01 — Genetics

The HLA-B*51 Story

From allele to antigen presentation — the strongest single genetic risk factor in autoimmune medicine

10.96×
Combined Odds Ratio — HLA-B*51 + ERAP1 Hap10
Takeuchi et al. (2016) — homozygous hypoactive ERAP1 (Hap10) co-occurring with HLA-B*51 confers OR 10.96 (95% CI 5.91–20.32; p = 4.80×10⁻¹⁰) versus individuals lacking both risk factors. ERAP1 risk is strictly absent in HLA-B*51-negative individuals — a purely epistatic modifier.

HLA-B*51 is the dominant genetic risk allele for Behçet's disease, with odds ratios of ~5–6× for heterozygotes. The allele sits in the MHC class I binding groove — it presents 8–10 amino acid peptides to CD8+ cytotoxic T cells. B*51's distinctive B pocket preferentially binds 9-mer peptides with Pro/Ala at P2 and hydrophobic C-terminal anchors.

Mechanism: ERAP1 trims N-terminal extensions of peptide precursors in the ER for MHC class I loading. The hypoactive Hap10 allotype generates a B*51 peptidome enriched in undertrimmed (>9-mer) peptides, altering which self-peptides reach the cell surface and priming aberrant CD8+ T cell responses. B*51 homozygosity doubles this signal on every nucleated cell. [Takeuchi 2016]

Section 02 — Pathogenesis

How B*51 Causes Disease — The Cascade

From aberrant peptide presentation to vascular endothelial damage

Step 1 — HLA-B*51/B*51 Homozygous
Double-dose aberrant peptide presentation on every nucleated cell. Self-peptides (HSP60, MICA, neurofilament NF-M, retinal S-antigen) and microbial peptides (S. sanguinis KTH-1, mycobacterial HSP65, HSV) cross-react via molecular mimicry.
↓
Step 2 — ERAP1 Epistasis (Hap10)
Hypoactive ERAP1 undertrimming generates an altered B*51 peptidome. OR for BD rises from ~4.5× (B*51 alone) to ~10.96× when ERAP1-Hap10 is also homozygous. ERAP1 risk is absent in B*51-negative individuals.
↓
Step 3 — Th1/Th17 Dual-Axis Activation
IL-12 → STAT4 → IFN-γ (Th1). IL-23 → IL-23R → RORγt → IL-17A/F (Th17). Dual activation drives the characteristic neutrophilic infiltrates in mucocutaneous and vascular BD. JAK inhibitors target here.
↓
Step 4 — Immune Checkpoint Failure
CTLA-4 and PD-1 brakes fail to suppress autoreactive T cells. IL-10 dysregulation reduces Treg suppression of the inflammatory cascade. The accelerator (B*51) outpaces weakened brakes.
↓
Step 5 — Vascular Endothelial Damage
BD is the only systemic vasculitis affecting both arteries and veins of all sizes. Neutrophil NETosis → direct endothelial cytotoxicity + thrombin generation. CD8+ T cells attack endothelium presenting cross-reactive peptides. IL-6 amplifies vascular inflammation. Result: oral ulcers, genital ulcers, uveitis, vascular thrombosis, neuro-BD, GI involvement.
Autoinflammatory pole — MEFV / Pyrin

B*51 × MEFV epistasis is statistically proven (p=0.003) in BD cohorts. M694V heterozygotes: OR 2.65–2.73 for BD in Turkish patients. MEFV gain-of-function lowers the pyrin inflammasome threshold → excess IL-1β + IL-18. MEFV-positive BD carries dramatically elevated complication rates: thrombosis 54% vs 17% (OR 6.9), neuro-BD in 70.6% of MEFV-positive neuro-BD patients. If MEFV-positive: IL-1 blockade (anakinra, canakinumab) becomes the targeted therapy. BD is increasingly classified as an MHC-I-opathy — a hybrid autoinflammatory/autoimmune condition.

Section 03 — Diagnosis

Diagnostic Criteria

ISG 1990 (specific, insensitive) vs. ICBD 2014 (point-based, more sensitive for incomplete disease)

ISG 1990 requires recurrent oral ulcers (≥3/year) PLUS two of: recurrent genital ulcers, ocular lesions (uveitis/retinal vasculitis), skin lesions (pseudofolliculitis, erythema nodosum), positive pathergy. High specificity; misses incomplete cases.

ICBD 2014 point-based scoring: oral ulcers (2), genital ulcers (2), ocular lesions (2), skin lesions (1), vascular involvement (1), positive pathergy (1). Score ≥4 = BD. Sensitivity ~94.8%, specificity ~90.5%. Better for early or incomplete presentations. [Fonollosa 2024]

Incomplete Behçet's / Complex Aphthosis

Patients with recurrent oral ulcers plus one or two features but not meeting full ISG criteria. Pediatric-onset cases often evolve over years — a patient with complex aphthosis at 17 may meet full criteria at 25. In Quinn's case: formal dx declined on incomplete criteria but clinical trajectory (pan-colonic ulceration, mucocutaneous disease, neurologic phase, HLA-B*51/B*51, Anatolian ancestry) is highly consistent with BD or a related neutrophilic mucosal disease.
