The Big Question — HLA-B*51
75% Chance He Carries B*51
Both parents are B*51 carriers. The math:

25%: B*51/B*51 homozygous — same as Quinn. OR ~10.96× for Behçet's. Would need the same surveillance and potentially the same interventions.

50%: B*51/other heterozygous — like both parents. OR ~5.78× Behçet's risk. Neither parent is symptomatic, but with the bilateral modifier gene stack (CTLA4, FUT2, ATG16L1, IL-1β all from both parents), heterozygous B*51 + modifiers is still a meaningful risk.

25%: No B*51 — genetically cleared of B*51-associated Behçet's risk. Still carries FUT2 non-secretor, FOXO3 TT, and all the cardiovascular/modifier variants, but without the autoimmune driver.

HLA typing is the single most important test for him.

His Role In the Family Investigation
The brother is a natural experiment. Same parents, different genetic dice roll. When his data arrives:

• If he's B*51 homozygous + no symptoms → environment (not just genes) is the key
• If he's B*51 heterozygous + no symptoms → gene DOSAGE is the answer (one copy vs two)
• If he's B*51 positive + has subtle symptoms → BOTH brothers affected, just different severity
• If he has NO B*51 → genetically cleared at the main driver locus

His data will also reveal which of the bilateral variants (CTLA4, ATG16L1, CYP2C9, PITX2, circadian genes) he inherited in the same combination as Quinn vs differently. Comparing both brothers' inheritance patterns against both parents will show WHY Quinn got sick and whether his brother is at risk.
