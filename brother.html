What to Watch For — Even Before Testing
While waiting for your genome data, be aware of these symptoms that could indicate early/subclinical Behçet's:

• Mouth ulcers — more than 3 per year, especially if they scar
• Skin problems — acne-like rash, bumps on shins (erythema nodosum), papules
• Eye redness or pain — especially if recurrent or with light sensitivity
• Joint pain or swelling — especially knees, ankles, wrists
• Genital ulcers
• Headaches — especially positional or with vision changes

If any of these sound familiar, tell your dad. It doesn't mean you have BD — but with B*51 in the family, it's worth checking.
