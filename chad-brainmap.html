AG
COMT Val/Met — The Balanced Mind
Val/Met = intermediate dopamine clearance. You sit between the "warrior" (Val/Val, fast clearance, stress-resilient but lower baseline dopamine) and the "worrier" (Met/Met, slow clearance, higher baseline but stress-sensitive). For neuroplasticity interventions, this means moderate, manageable experience intensity — neither overwhelming nor underwhelming. The Goldilocks genotype.
Research: Studerus et al., 2012 (Psychopharmacology) — COMT Val158Met predicts subjective response to psilocybin. Barrett et al., 2020 (J Psychopharmacol) — COMT genotype modulates mystical-type experiences.
For Quinn: If you inherited the Met allele from dad (50% chance) or from mom (Brigitte is COMT AA = Met/Met), you'd likely have a balanced-to-sensitive response profile. Brigitte guarantees you at least one Met — you're either Val/Met like dad or Met/Met like mom.
CT
BDNF Val66Met — The Neuroplasticity Window
Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor is the master growth signal for neurons. Met carriers have lower baseline BDNF secretion — which counterintuitively means more room for upward neuroplastic response. The mechanism: psychedelics promote dendritic spine growth via the BDNF-TrkB signaling pathway. Starting from a lower baseline, Met carriers show greater relative neurogenesis gains. Think of it as having more ceiling to grow into.
Research: Ly et al., 2018 (Cell Reports) — psychedelics promote structural/functional neural plasticity. Shao et al., 2021 (Neuropharmacology) — BDNF Val66Met modulates psilocybin-induced neuroplasticity. Castrén & Monteggia, 2021 (Biol Psychiatry) — BDNF-TrkB as target for neuropsychiatric interventions.
For Quinn: 50% chance you carry Met. If so, a single neuroplasticity intervention could have outsized benefit — your neurons are primed for growth. Even without psychedelics, exercise-induced BDNF release (the "runner's high") hits Met carriers harder.
CT
5-HT2A Receptor (HTR2A) — The Target Receptor
This is literally the receptor that psilocybin binds to. The 5-HT2A serotonin receptor is the primary pharmacological target of all classic psychedelics. rs6311 CT = heterozygous = moderate receptor density in the prefrontal cortex. This means a balanced binding profile — enough receptors for meaningful activation, not so many that it's overwhelming. The lock is medium-density. Psilocybin is the key.
Research: Ott et al., 2022 (Neuropsychopharmacology) — HTR2A polymorphisms predict subjective psychedelic intensity. de la Fuente Revenga et al., 2021 (Cell) — 5-HT2A receptor-dependent structural plasticity underlies psychedelic effects. Madsen et al., 2019 (Biol Psychiatry) — PET imaging of 5-HT2A binding and psilocybin response.
For Quinn: You inherited either C or T from dad at this position. Combined with mom's allele, your receptor density determines how intensely you'd respond to any serotonergic compound — including SSRIs, not just psychedelics.

The Science of Neuroplasticity
You don't need psychedelics to activate these genes
Exercise — Increases BDNF 2-3x acutely. ACTN3 TT endurance genotype means sustained aerobic activity = sustained BDNF elevation. The most powerful neuroplasticity tool you already have. Zone 2 for 45+ minutes triggers peak BDNF release.

Meditation — Increases 5-HT2A receptor density over time (the same receptor psychedelics target). 8 weeks of mindfulness measurably changes prefrontal cortex structure. COMT Val/Met: you're genetically suited for balanced meditative states.

Cold exposure — 11 minutes/week of deliberate cold (Huberman protocol) increases norepinephrine 2-3x and upregulates BDNF. DBH TT from Brigitte (low norepinephrine) makes this especially valuable for Quinn.

Sleep — Deep sleep is when BDNF consolidates new synaptic connections. APOE ε4 makes this non-negotiable — glymphatic clearance happens during N3/REM. 7-8 hours minimum.

Fasting — 16-18 hour fasts activate AMPK and autophagy, promoting neural cleanup and BDNF expression. FOXO3 TT (no longevity allele) means you need these environmental activators more than most.

Learning — Novel skill acquisition is the original neuroplasticity intervention. Musical instruments, new languages, complex movement patterns. BDNF Met carriers show enhanced learning-dependent plasticity.
Bottom line: Knowing your genotype helps optimize ANY neuroplasticity intervention. Exercise + meditation + cold + sleep + fasting is a daily neuroplasticity stack that activates every gene on this page — no prescription required.

A Note on Timing
Information for the future, not a recommendation for today
The prefrontal cortex — the part of the brain responsible for judgment, impulse control, and long-term planning — continues developing until approximately age 25. The same neuroplasticity that makes these interventions potentially transformative also makes a developing brain more sensitive to perturbation.

For Quinn: The genetic profile is favorable. The science is promising. But the timeline matters. The 5-HT2A receptors, the BDNF pathway, the COMT balance — they'll all still be there. Dad's genome data will still be here. This page will still be here.

In the meantime, every item in the non-psychedelic neuroplasticity stack (exercise, meditation, cold, sleep, learning) activates the exact same pathways through the exact same genes. You can build your brain right now, with what you have, at the pace that's right for you.
