#1 — Critical / Flag in EMR
CYP2C9 *1/*2 — Pharmacogenomic-Guided Warfarin Dosing Required
rs1799853 CT. Intermediate metabolizer — CYP2C9 *2 allele reduces S-warfarin metabolism ~30-40%. CPIC guideline (active 2025): *1/*2 alone does not mandate a blanket dose reduction. Instead, use the IWPC pharmacogenetic dosing algorithm incorporating VKORC1-1639G>A alongside CYP2C9 *2 for individualized dosing. Blanket reductions are reserved for *2/*2, *1/*3, *2/*3, *3/*3. Also affects NSAIDs (higher plasma levels at standard doses). Action: Flag in EMR. Insist on IWPC-guided dosing with VKORC1 genotype before any warfarin. Prefer DOACs (apixaban/rivaroxaban) — no CYP2C9 interaction. Inform anesthesia and all treating physicians.

The 9p21 locus is the most replicated genetic risk factor for coronary artery disease. Chad carries heterozygous risk alleles at all three independent 9p21 signals, conferring ~1.4-1.7× elevated MI risk. This is independent of traditional risk factors. Action: Coronary artery calcium (CAC) score at age 40-45, aggressive LDL target (<70 mg/dL), statin consideration even at normal LDL. Annual CV risk calculation.
#3 — Key Discovery

#4 — Elevated Risk
APOE ε3/ε4 — 3× Alzheimer's Risk
rs429358 CT. The strongest genetic risk factor for late-onset Alzheimer's. One copy of ε4 elevates lifetime risk to ~15-25% (vs 9% baseline). Onset risk rises from age 40 for ε4 carriers. 2025 RCT data (EXERT trial and Finnish FINGER-2 data): structured aerobic exercise ≥150 min/week reduces cognitive decline trajectory in ε4 carriers significantly. Action: Start Alzheimer's prevention protocol now. Exercise ≥150 min/week vigorous cardio, 7-9h sleep, MIND diet, DHA 1g/day, control BP <120/80.
#5 — Key Discovery

CRITICAL NUANCE: serum B12 may paradoxically appear normal or HIGH in non-secretors — this reflects impaired cellular transport, not sufficiency. Annual MMA + homocysteine — do NOT rely on serum B12 alone. Probiotic with Bifidobacterium longum.

Novel Genetic Interactions
⚡ APOE ε4 + CYP2C9 *1/*2 + IL-6 CC — The Inflammation-Alzheimer's Trap
These three variants create a vicious cycle: IL-6 CC elevates neuroinflammation, which accelerates amyloid deposition in APOE ε4 carriers. Meanwhile, CYP2C9 *1/*2 means NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) — which could theoretically reduce neuroinflammation — accumulate at higher plasma levels, risking GI and renal side effects at doses needed for anti-inflammatory effect.

Clinical implication: Avoid chronic NSAID use for anti-inflammatory purposes. Instead use omega-3 EPA/DHA (2g/day), curcumin, and aerobic exercise as safer anti-inflammatory strategies. Consider low-dose aspirin risk/benefit carefully with CYP2C9 discussion.
⚡ Triple MC1R + FUT2 Non-Secretor — Skin Immunity Interaction
FUT2 non-secretor status alters gut microbiome composition (dramatically reduced Bifidobacterium), which affects systemic immune function and tumor surveillance. Combined with triple MC1R (impaired UV-DNA repair signaling), this creates a double vulnerability: impaired local UV defense AND potentially reduced systemic immune surveillance. 2024 research shows gut microbiome diversity correlates with melanoma immunotherapy response.

Clinical implication: Probiotic supplementation (Bifidobacterium longum specifically) may support both gut and immune health. Fermented foods daily. This combination makes strict sun protection non-negotiable.
⚡ ACTN3 XX + AMPD1 AG + PPARGC1A CT — The Endurance Profile
ACTN3 XX removes fast-twitch fiber limitation, AMPD1 AG maintains normal purine nucleotide cycle (no exercise intolerance), and PPARGC1A CT provides intermediate mitochondrial biogenesis capacity. Together these define an endurance athlete who responds best to aerobic training, improves steadily with volume, but won't maximize power output.

Training implication: Zone 2 (MAF method) 3-4x/week builds the PPARGC1A mitochondrial response. Add 1-2 VO2max sessions/week. Strength training improves connective tissue (COL5A1 CT) — prioritize eccentric loading for tendon adaptation.
⚡ FUT2 Non-Secretor + MTHFR Compound + MTNR1B CG — Metabolic Timing Matters
FUT2 AA impairs B12 absorption. MTHFR compound reduces methylfolate synthesis. MTNR1B CG means late-night eating elevates glucose (melatonin suppresses insulin secretion in CG carriers). This triad means: nutrient timing, consistent meal patterns, and early eating windows are genetically mandated for metabolic health.

Protocol: Eat within a 10-12h window (7am-7pm optimal). No large meals after 7pm. Take methylfolate + methylcobalamin with breakfast (B12 absorption aided by stomach acid stimulation from food).
⚡ 9p21 CAD + PITX2 AFib + ADRB1 CG — The Cardiovascular Constellation
Three independent cardiovascular genetic signals converge: 9p21 elevates atherosclerosis risk, PITX2 elevates AFib risk, and ADRB1 CG affects heart rate response and beta-blocker pharmacology. AFib on a background of coronary disease creates anticoagulation complexity — and CYP2C9 *1/*2 makes warfarin dangerous at standard doses.

Clinical plan: If AFib develops, DOAC (apixaban preferred) over warfarin. Exercise aggressively — it reduces both AFib and CAD risk simultaneously. Annual EKG + lipid panel starting now.
